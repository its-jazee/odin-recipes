Cuttlefish Sambal (Sambal Sotong)

Sambal Sotong (translated to Cuttlefish Sambal or Cuttlefish Chilli) is one of the many dishes from Malaysia. It is a nostalgic cuisine that consist of simple ingredients. There are many proteins that can be cooked into a sambal style dish, but today, we will focus on one of my favourite protein that is well-known by the Malaysian community. Sotong rendam is a dried cuttlefish that is rehydrated and is usually cooked in sambal to compliment it's bland taste.
(Credits to my sister for the family recipe and Azie Kitchen for the picture)

Ingredients :

  • cuttlefish
  • red onion
  • garlic
  • chilli paste
  • anchovy paste

Steps :

  1. Cut up the cuttlefish to bite size pieces (around the size of half your index finger).
  2. Chop the garlic and onion finely.
  3. Heat up some oil in the pan and saute the chopped onion and garlic until they turn brown.
  4. Add in the chilli paste and saute until the chilli smell disintegrates.
  5. Add the chopped cuttlefish and add some water to form the broth.
  6. Add some anchovy paste.
  7. Stir and salt to taste once the cuttlefish is cooked.
  8. Plate up and enjoy.
